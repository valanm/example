Why accelerating taxonomy?

If we are to complete inventory of life and to save this biodiversity for upcoming generations we need to accelerate taxonomic delivery and make relevant information easily accessible to everyone.

How?

Our goal

To explore the utility of cutting-edge image recognition technology focusing on a common and diverse but poorly known insect group, the parasitic-wasp family Ichneumonidae (estimates up to 100 000 species).

Something like face recognition on Facebook but we will use wings instead of faces.

Workflow

Iteratively

each iteration consists of the following six steps

  • Step 1

    Collect specimens

    The primary source of specimens for the image acquisition is the SMTP material, which includes millions of ichneumonid specimens in alcohol, sorted into 50 taxonomic fractions by experts.

  • Step 2

    Images aquiring

    One side forewing and hindwing detached and mounted on microscope slides or by holding the wing between two microscope slides.

  • Step 3

    Image preprocessing

    This step will be done using OpenCV, an open source library for Computer Vision

  • Step 4

    Train the machine

    To train the machine to recognise objects and details in the picture we need lot of training data (images), sufficient learning algorithms (DEEP LEARNING) with sufficient computational power (GPU).

  • Step 5

    Classification

    Classification involves dividing the data into various interrelated classes.

  • Step 6

    Verification

    To verify if the machine clasified images properly, identification is required for all the specimens used in the study.

  • repeat
    with new set
    of images
